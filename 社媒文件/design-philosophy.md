# Pale Blueprint

Pale Blueprint treats each poster as a calibrated research instrument held between two dark institutional bands. The header and footer are fixed navy anchors for mobile-app safety; the middle is a pale blue field where workflow, evidence, and submission structure can be read quickly. The work should feel meticulously crafted, with every margin, line, and label adjusted like a journal production proof rather than a casual social graphic.

The palette is intentionally restrained but not one-note. Navy carries authority, pale blue carries the workspace, electric blue marks active routes, maroon marks hard decisions, and a small orange accent keeps the system human. These accents are used sparingly and with painstaking attention, so the series looks professionally labored over instead of decorative.

The visual language comes from empirical research infrastructure: stage passports, method gates, evidence ledgers, backend routing, and submission packs. These ideas should appear as diagrams, tables, rails, chips, and compact cards, not as explanatory paragraphs. Information is carried first by spatial order and hierarchy; text acts as an engraved label on an instrument.

Typography must be phone-first. The header names the series, the footer gives the repository, and the body carries one strong claim plus only the labels needed to understand the graphic. Chinese headlines carry the editorial argument; monospace English anchors the repo mechanics. Every line must sit inside its own clear boundary with no collisions, no cramped text, and no accidental overlap.

Across the five pages, variation comes from structure rather than palette drift: a workflow engine, two hard gates, a ledger, a three-backend router, and a submission pack. The result should feel like a single polished campaign: consistent enough to swipe as a set, but distinct enough that each page has its own research-grade action.
